Busan

Busan, the rising star of South Korea, offers everything from top-notch barbecue restaurants to traditional street food stalls. Here, we explore South Korea's second city and discover its culinary offerings

For the history lovers

Discover 3 must-visit destinations in Busan.

The attractions of Busan range from centuries-old Buddhist temples dotting the city's mountains and coastline to pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. This coastal city offers plenty of things to do year-round – families can spend time at a seaside aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant neighborhoods, and nature lovers can enjoy long hikes to panoramic viewpoints. The Buddhist sanctuaries scattered along Busan's coast and mountains feature impressive architecture that will delight photographers.

  1. Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

    2. Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the northeasternmost part of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few temples in Korea constructed by the seaside—you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other.

    Good to:

    • History
  2. Beomeo-sa Temple

    3. Beomeosa Temple is one of the largest sanctuaries in South Korea. It is situated high on the eastern edge of Geumjeong Mountain, far from the hustle and bustle of the city. The temple's Daeungjeon Hall is a well-preserved example of Joseon Dynasty architecture.

    Good to:

    • History
  3. Yongdusan Park

    4. Yongdusan Park, located in the heart of Busan, is home to some of the city's most important monuments. You can enjoy spectacular views from the top of the 120-meter-high Busan Tower. The park has two museums—check out the traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and over 80 Korean sailboats at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats.

    Good to:

    • Couples
    • Families
    • Budget

The best things to do in Busan highlight the city's reputation as a major seaport in Asia. Often seen as the essence of South Korea, you'll experience a unique atmosphere in terms of ethnic and cultural diversity, as the city attracts a cosmopolitan crowd year-round.
